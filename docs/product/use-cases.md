# Use Cases

## Target Users

SuperPlanify is designed for both individuals and organizations that need to create, manage, or consume schedules.

Typical users include:

* Students
* Teachers
* Employees
* Managers
* Sports clubs
* Driving schools
* Associations
* Training centers
* Small and medium-sized businesses
* Any organization that coordinates people through schedules

The platform serves two complementary audiences:

* **Members**, who consume schedules created by organizations.
* **Administrators**, who create and manage organizations, schedules, and participants.

---

# Version 1 Scope

The first version focuses on solving a simple but common problem:

* Organizations can create and publish schedules.
* Members can access all their schedules from a single application.

The goal is to validate the core scheduling experience before introducing more advanced collaboration and workflow features.

---

## Use Case 1 – Unified Personal Schedule

### Actor

Organization member.

### Goal

Access every schedule from every organization in one place.

### Scenario

A user is enrolled at a university, attends a sports club, and takes driving lessons.

Instead of checking three different websites or applications, the user signs in to SuperPlanify and immediately sees every organization they belong to.

The user can:

* browse available organizations;
* open each schedule;
* view upcoming time slots;
* identify who created an event;
* see who is participating.

SuperPlanify becomes the user's single entry point for schedule consultation.

---

## Use Case 2 – Create and Manage an Organization

### Actor

Organization administrator.

### Goal

Create and publish schedules for an organization.

### Scenario

An administrator creates a new organization.

They can:

* create one or more schedules;
* define time slots;
* invite members;
* assign members to schedules.

Members immediately gain read-only access to the schedules they are assigned to.

The administrator does not need to rely on spreadsheets, messaging applications, or custom internal tools to distribute scheduling information.

---

## Out of Scope (Version 1)

The following features are intentionally excluded from the first version:

* Fine-grained role and permission management.
* Collaborative schedule editing.
* Polls and voting.
* Real-time notifications.
* Advanced scheduling workflows.
* Automated decision-making.
* Organization hierarchies.

These capabilities are planned for future iterations once the core scheduling workflow has been validated.
